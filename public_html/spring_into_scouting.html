Why Join Scouting?
Scouting builds confidence, leadership, and outdoor skills while creating unforgettable experiences. From hiking and camping to fire-building and teamwork, your child will gain hands-on skills that last a lifetime.

What to Expect at Spring into Scouting?

  • Fun Outdoor Activities – Learn fire-starting, rope-tying, and first aid basics!
  • Meet the Scouts – Talk to current Scouts and hear about their adventures!
  • Parent Info Session – Learn how Scouting helps kids grow into leaders!
  • Try It Out – Your child can jump in and experience Scouting firsthand!

Who Can Join?
Boys and girls in 5th grade through 17 years-old who are ready for fun, adventure, and learning valuable life skills. No prior Scouting experience is needed!

Parents, We’ve Got You Covered!
Scouting isn’t just about kids – parents love it too! It’s a safe, structured, and engaging program that helps children gain independence while building strong values. Plus, you’ll have a great community of supportive families to connect with.

To be eligible, youth must meet one of the following criteria:

  • Age: Be at least 11 years old, but not yet 18.
  • Grade: Be at least 10 years old, currently in the fifth grade, and register on or after March 1st.
  • Arrow of Light: Have earned the Arrow of Light Award (the highest rank in Cub Scouting) and be at least 10 years old.

This means that fifth-grade students who are at least 10 years old can join Scouts BSA starting March 1st, even if they haven’t yet turned 11 or completed the fifth grade. Additionally, those who have earned the Arrow of Light Award and are at least 10 years old are eligible to join at any time.

For more detailed information, please refer to the official Scouting America FAQ page.
